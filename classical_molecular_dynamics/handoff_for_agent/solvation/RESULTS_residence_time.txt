Mg-anion ion-pair residence time -- intermittent survival ACF (Impey-Madden)
============================================================================
Prompted by the solvation/desolvation review (coordination EXCHANGE RATE governs
sigma/t+). Cutoff Mg-anionCl 0.345 nm; window 50-100 ns @ 200 ps (251 frames).
C(tau) = < |pairs(t) ∩ pairs(t+tau)| / |pairs(t)| >.

  system        <pairs/frame>   C(1 ns)   C(5 ns)   tau_c(1/e)
  bare    r1        151.1         0.971     0.921     > 25 ns
  bare    r2        153.3         0.966     0.904     > 25 ns
  swollen8 r1       133.0         0.980     0.955     > 25 ns
  swollen8 r2       133.3         0.988     0.972     > 25 ns

Interpretation
--------------
* Mg-anion contact pairs are VERY long-lived in BOTH systems: the ACF barely
  decays over 25 ns (C(5ns) ~ 0.90-0.97), tau_c(1/e) is only a LOWER BOUND (>25 ns,
  i.e. >> the transport timescale). These strongly-bound divalent complexes do NOT
  exchange ion-pair partners on the conduction timescale.
* swollen pairs are if anything MORE persistent than bare (C(5ns) 0.96-0.97 vs
  0.90-0.92) -- the gel does NOT accelerate coordination exchange; it slightly slows it.
* Fewer pairs in swollen (~133 vs ~151 per 160 Mg) -- consistent with CIP->SSIP
  de-pairing (SSIP/CIP/AGG analysis: 84% vs 95% CIP).

Mechanistic conclusion
----------------------
The swollen-gel conductivity gain is CARRIER-NUMBER-driven (thermodynamic de-pairing,
more SSIP), NOT exchange-rate-driven. In the review's framing these Mg complexes sit in
the 'rigid / slow-exchange, whole-entity movement' regime, NOT the 'fast coordination
exchange / hopping' regime that benefits Li+. Therefore the swollen ÷1.6 mobility drop
is NOT caused by changed ion-pair kinetics (pairs are kinetically frozen in both) -> it
points to network confinement / tortuosity / friction (a Story C / mechanics question),
not to ion-pair dynamics. Caveat: intermittent ACF + 25 ns max lag give a lower bound on
tau_c; a continuous-lifetime definition over longer windows would sharpen the absolute value.
