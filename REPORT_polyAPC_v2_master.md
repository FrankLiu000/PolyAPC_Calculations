## Executive summary — the honest, evidence-based picture (v2)
The v1 study established a conclusion that still stands: **poly-APC is not better at moving Mg²⁺** (≈4.4× slower in bulk, t₊ = 0.50 unchanged, cation segregated ~7 Å from the network); the win is **mechanical / interfacial / safety**, not transport. v2 adds the chemistry v1 omitted — the **redox chemistry of the APC aluminium anion and the (poly-specific) triflate-derived fluoride SEI** — because the XPS shows these are first-order interfacial effects.

The headline observable v2 must explain is the **3.1 eV Al 2p split**: bare-APC 70.88 eV (anomalously low → reduced / Mg–Al-alloyed Al⁰) vs poly-APC 73.98 eV (oxidised Al³⁺). Working hypothesis, tested below: **the bare anode reductively decomposes the Al anion and co-deposits Al (as Mg–Al alloy), roughening Mg and seeding soft-shorts; the cured network immobilises/positions the anion away from the reductive plating front, suppressing Al co-deposition and leaving a benign Al³⁺ interphase.**

**Methods (consistent across v1→v2).** Molecular: B3LYP-D3(BJ)/def2-TZVP//def2-SVP, SMD(THF), int=ultrafine; redox/EA cross-checked with ωB97X-D & M06-2X, anion EAs with diffuse def2-TZVPD; Multiwfn ESP/Fukui/spin/QTAIM. Periodic/AIMD: CP2K PBE-D3, DZVP-MOLOPT-SR-GTH + GTH-PBE, CUTOFF 400/REL 50; Mg(0001) slab (Φ=3.97 eV validated). Interface electron transfer computed **both** ways (CDFT/Marcus and Dirichlet-BC fixed-potential) and cross-compared.

**Scope note (locked decisions).** Full program, Phases 0–4. Bare-APC modelled **F-free**: the MgF₂/triflate SEI is treated as **poly-specific**; bare's ~4.3 % F is flagged as a contamination/handling artifact and the **missing S 2p scan** is flagged. Interface ET cross-compared by two methods. Full deliverable set (data + structures + report + figures + reconciliation table).
